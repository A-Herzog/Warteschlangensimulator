Meaning of the columns in this table:

  • Station or client type: Model station or client type to which the respective row refers.
  • Number: Number of client events at this station during the whole simulation.
  • E[.]: Average time distance (in seconds) between two corresponding events at this station.
  • Std[.]: Standard deviation of the time distances (in seconds) between two corresponding events at this station.
  • Var[.]: Variance of the time distances (in seconds) between two corresponding events at this station.
  • CV[.]: Coefficient of variation of the time distances between two corresponding events at this station.
  • Sk[.]: Skewness of the time distances between two corresponding events at this station.
  • Min[.]: Minimal time distance (in seconds) between two corresponding events at this station.
  • Max[.]: Maximal time distance (in seconds) between two corresponding events at this station.
  • 10% Quantil[.]: 10% quantile of the time distances between two corresponding events at this station. 10% of the time intervals were at most as long (in seconds) as shown in this cell.
  • 25 Quantil[.]: 25% quantile of the time distances between two corresponding events at this station. 25% of the time intervals were at most as long (in seconds) as shown in this cell.
  • 50% Quantil[.]: 50% quantile of the time distances between two corresponding events at this station. 50% of the time intervals were at most as long (in seconds) as shown in this cell. This value is also the median of the times distances.
  • 75% Quantil[.]: 75% quantile of the time distances between two corresponding events at this station. 75% of the time intervals were at most as long (in seconds) as shown in this cell.
  • 90% Quantil[.]: 90% quantile of the time distances between two corresponding events at this station. 90% of the time intervals were at most as long (in seconds) as shown in this cell.
