Number of arrivals

This section shows how many clients have arrived at the respective stations during the entire simulation. Since the simulation is terminated at a point in time when clients are in the system (at different stations), even in a strictly linear model the results for the number of arrived clients at the different station can slightly differ.

Inter-arrival times

An inter-arrival time is the time span between the arrival of two immediately consecutive clients at a station. Average value, standard deviation, variances, coefficients of variation and quantiles are displayed.

The average inter-arrival time is designated by E[I] (and is measured in seconds or minutes, for example). The reciprocal of E[I] is the arrival rate. It is designated by lambda (λ) (measured in e.g. 1/second or 1/minute). Small values of E[I] or large values of lambda mean that many clients arrive within a short time.
