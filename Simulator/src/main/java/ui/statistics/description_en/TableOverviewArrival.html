Meaning of the columns in this table:

  • Station or client type: Model station at which the clients have arrived or client type to which the data in this row referes.
  • Number: Number of arrivals at this station during the whole simulation.
  • E[I]: Average time distance (in seconds) between two arrivals at this station.
  • Std[I]: Standard deviation of the time distances (in seconds) between two arrivals at this station.
  • Var[I]: Variance of the time distances (in seconds) between two arrivals at this station.
  • CV[I]: Coefficient of variation of the time distances between two arrivals at this station.
  • Sk[I]: Skewness of the time distances between two arrivals at this station.
  • Kurt[I]: Excess kurtosis of the time distances between two arrivals at this station.
  • Min[I]: Minimal time distance (in seconds) between two arrivals at this station.
  • Max[I]: Maximal time distance (in seconds) between two arrivals at this station.
  • 10% Quantil[I]: 10% quantile of the time distances between two arrivals at this station. 10% of the time intervals were at most as long (in seconds) as shown in this cell.
  • 25 Quantil[I]: 25% quantile of the time distances between two arrivals at this station. 25% of the time intervals were at most as long (in seconds) as shown in this cell.
  • 50% Quantil[I]: 50% quantile of the time distances between two arrivals at this station. 50% of the time intervals were at most as long (in seconds) as shown in this cell. This value is also the median of the inter-arrival times.
  • 75% Quantil[I]: 75% quantile of the time distances between two arrivals at this station. 75% of the time intervals were at most as long (in seconds) as shown in this cell.
  • 90% Quantil[I]: 90% quantile of the time distances between two arrivals at this station. 90% of the time intervals were at most as long (in seconds) as shown in this cell.
