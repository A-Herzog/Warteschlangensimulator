Allen-Cunneen

The Allen-Cunneen formula, in contrast to the Erlang formulas, is only an approximate formula. This means that the results that this formula provides will not be absolutely identical to reality, even if all the conditions for using the formula are met.

In contrast to the Erlang formulas, the Allen-Cunneen queueing model no longer assumes that inter-arrival times and the service times are distributed exponentially. Instead, any distributions are now allowed, which are characterized only by their expected values (λ or μ as reciprocals of the expected values) and their coefficients of variation (CV[I] and CV[S]). Furthermore, the clients can arrive in groups (arrival batch size b(I)) and be served in groups (service batch size b(S)).

Performance indicators

The following parameters are calculated on the basis of the inputs:

  • Offered work load a: This value indicates how many operators are needed in any case to serve the arriving clients on a long-term average. The offered work load is the quotient of the arrival and service rate: a=λ/μ.
  • Work load ρ: The work load is the quotient of offered work load and the specified number of operators (ρ=a/c). The work load must always be <1, so that the operators can serve the arriving clients on a long-term average. However, a work load of less than 1 alone is not a statement that the system is sensibly dimensioned, since the work load does not provide any information about how long the clients will have to wait on average in the system.
  • Average queue length E[NQ]: The average queue length indicates the average number of clients waiting (i.e. not yet served) in a correspondingly configured system.
  • Average number of clients in the system E[N]: This value indicates the average total number of clients in the system, i.e. it is the sum of waiting and currently served clients.
  • Average waiting time E[W]: Using Little's formula, the average queue length E [NQ] (and the arrival rate λ) can be used to calculate how long clients will have to wait on average before they are served.
  • Average residence time E[V]: The residence time is the sum of the average waiting time E[W] and the average service time E[S]. The average service time is the reciprocal of the service rate (E[S]=1/μ).
