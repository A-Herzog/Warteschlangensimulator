Work load

The work load ρ of a queueing system is the quotient of the arrival rate λ on the one hand and the product of service rate μ and the number of operators c on the other.

In order for a queueing system to be stable in the long term, ρ<1 must apply, i.e. fewer clients must arrive than the operators can serve.

If one resolves the formula ρ=λ/(c·μ) to c, the result is: c=λ/(ρ·μ). In this way, it is possible to determine how many operators are required to achieve a given work load with a known arrival and a known service rate.

Limits of the calculation

This calculation does not take into account that the actual arrivals are at random times and that the actual service times vary in length. Therefore, the above calculation does not provide any information about how long the waiting times would be in such a system.
