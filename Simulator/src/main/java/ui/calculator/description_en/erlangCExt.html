Erlang C (extended)

The extended Erlang-C formula extends the modelling scope of the Erlang-C formula to include wait cancellers, which are caused by a limited waiting time tolerance of the clients. To use the enhanced Erlang-C formula, you need a model which is structured as follows:

  • Exponential distributed inter-arrival times
  • Exponential distributed service times
  • Exponential distributed waiting time tolerances
  • A fixed number of operators
  • No other special properties

Performance indicators

The following parameters are calculated on the basis of the inputs:

  • Offered work load a: This value indicates how many operators are needed in any case to serve the arriving clients on a long-term average in case there is not waiting time cancelation. The offered work load is the quotient of the arrival and service rate: a=λ/μ.
  • Work load ρ: The work load is the quotient of offered work load and the specified number of operators (ρ=a/c). The work load must always be <1, so that the operators can serve the arriving clients on a long-term average. However, a work load of less than 1 alone is not a statement that the system is sensibly dimensioned, since the work load does not provide any information about how long the clients will have to wait on average in the system.
  • Average queue length E[NQ]: The average queue length indicates the average number of clients waiting (i.e. not yet served) in a correspondingly configured system.
  • Average number of clients in the system E[N]: This value indicates the average total number of clients in the system, i.e. it is the sum of waiting and currently served clients.
  • Average waiting time E[W]: Using Little's formula, the average queue length E [NQ] (and the arrival rate λ) can be used to calculate how long clients will have to wait on average before they are served.
  • Average residence time E[V]: The residence time is the sum of the average waiting time E[W] and the average service time E[S]. The average service time is the reciprocal of the service rate (E[S]=1/μ).
  • Cancel rate P(A): This value indicates the percentage of clients who give up waiting early and leave the system without being served.
  • Service level P(W≤t): Frequently, one is not only interested in the average waiting time of clients, but also in the proportion of clients who had to wait for a certain, predetermined period of time at most. This information is provided by the service level. P(W≤t) is the proportion of clients who had to wait at most t seconds.

Criticism

In contrast to a simulation, the extended Erlang-C formula provides exact results with a very low computational effort. However, the results only describe reality accurately if the above properties are exactly fulfilled. Especially the following properties are often critical in reality:

  • The service times are usually not exponentially distributed, but are subject to a different distribution with usually a different coefficient of variation than is the case with the exponential distribution (there always CV[S]=1).
  • Several clients are served simultaneously (batch service), the clients are divided into different types with different service times or the number of operators varies over time.
  • Also network effects ( waiting cancelers start another attempt later, already served clients are referred again to the queue or several stations play together) are not mapped by the Erlang-C formula.
